2. Foundational Business Concepts

Cap Table

A capitalization table showing who owns what. Current state:

When investment is taken, new rows are added. If Gregg invests $50K at a $2M valuation, he'd own ~2.5%.

SAFE Note

Simple Agreement for Future Equity (created by Y Combinator). Investor gives money now; it converts into ownership at the next funding round. Key terms: Valuation Cap (maximum price for conversion) and Discount (typically 20% off next round price). Note: SAFEs are designed for C-Corps. Since Sagemind is an LLC, a convertible note is likely the better instrument.

LLC vs C-Corp

LLCC-Corp (Delaware)
Investment compatibilityHarder — can't use standard SAFEsStandard — all investor docs work
Tax treatmentPass-throughDouble taxation, but QSBS benefits
QSBS benefitNot availableUp to $10M capital gains tax-free
Conversion costN/A$2K–$5K legal fees
Best forSingle angel, staying smallMultiple investors, planning to scale

Bottom line: For a single angel like Gregg, LLC works. For future VC rounds, convert to Delaware C-Corp.

Valuation

Pre-money: What the company is worth before investment. Post-money: Pre-money + investment.

At Sagemind's stage, valuation is negotiated. Inputs: revenue multiple ($100K run rate × 10–20x = $1M–$2M), Berkus method (~$1.5M), comparable companies ($1M–$3M). Reasonable range: $1.5M–$2.5M pre-money.

Convertible Note

A loan that converts into ownership later. Compatible with LLC structure. Interest rate (5–8%), maturity (18–24 months), converts at a discount when you raise a bigger round. Delays the hard valuation conversation.

60% of a $10M company is worth far more than 100% of a $100K company. Dilution is the cost of growth.

Option Pool & Founder Vesting

Option pool: 10–20% reserved for future employees. Founder vesting: Standard 4-year vest, 1-year cliff. Since Justin has been working for a while, use "reverse vesting" to credit time served.

5. Business Plan (Lean)

Executive Summary

Sagemind AI LLC helps small and mid-sized businesses integrate AI into their operations — custom agents, automated workflows, and AI-powered websites at a fraction of enterprise consultancy pricing. Sagemind owns Bot-Me (SaaS platform) and Dawn (AI conversation product, 200+ users). Generated $46.9K in 2025, on pace for ~$100K in 2026 with 60% net margins. Seeking angel investment for hardware, legal formalization, and scaling through the Vistage CEO network.

Problem

  • SMBs are being left behind — enterprise consultancies charge $300–500/hr
  • Off-the-shelf AI tools are too generic for real business workflows
  • SMB owners know they need AI but don't know where to start
  • Traditional SaaS model is being disrupted — rigid, expensive, hard to customize

Solution

  1. AI Integration Consulting — Custom agents and automations for each business's workflows
  2. Bot-Me Platform (owned SaaS) — Multi-tenant AI assistant platform
  3. Dawn (owned product) — AI conversation product with persistent memory

Differentiators: AI-native delivery (3–5x faster), milestone pricing (outcomes, not hours), reusable across verticals, owned platform.
