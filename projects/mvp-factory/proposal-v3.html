3 How It Works — The Client Journey

The process is designed to be fast for us, easy for them. From first contact to live site typically takes 5–10 business days, gated almost entirely by how quickly the client sends their content.

1

The Spec Pitch

Adriana sends a personalized outreach message with a link to the live spec site. The prospect can see exactly what their site would look like — no imagination required. "Here's your bakery's website. I built it already."

2

Discovery Call (30 min)

Justin or Adriana walks the prospect through the demo site, answers questions, and confirms the scope: what's included, what they need to provide, how the handoff works, and the $1,999 flat fee. No proposals, no back-and-forth.

3

Content Gathering

Client pays the 50% non-refundable deposit via Stripe ($999.50). We send a simple content checklist: business name, logo/photos, menu or service list, contact info, and Google Maps link. Client has 30 days to send content — if they don't, the project goes on hold.

4

Build V1 (4–8 hours dev time)

We update the config file with client content, swap in their photos, and tune the copy. No rebuilding from scratch — we're editing a known-working template. When V1 is ready, we deploy a preview URL for the client to review.

5

Two Revision Rounds

Client reviews the preview and provides feedback. We incorporate changes. Two rounds of revisions are included in the flat fee. Additional revisions after that are billed at the hourly add-on rate.

6

Handoff & Final Payment

Client pays the remaining 50% ($999.50). We deploy the final site to their own Vercel account, connect their domain (they buy it; we walk them through it), hand over a zip of the complete codebase, and send a brief "You own it" documentation package. Sagemind keeps a private backup repo forever — that's it. Done.

Important: Sagemind Never Buys Anything for the Client

Domains, API keys, Vercel accounts — the client sets these up themselves. We walk them through it, but every account is in their name, with their payment method. This protects Sagemind from liability and makes the ownership transfer clean.

10 Launch Strategy — First Clients Are Free

The biggest barrier to selling websites is the lack of a real portfolio with real testimonials from real local businesses. The fastest way to solve this is to give the first few sites away — not as a discount, but as a deliberate strategic investment.

The Free Client Program

The first 3–5 clients receive their site at no charge. In exchange, they sign a written agreement providing: (1) a written testimonial and permission to use it in marketing, (2) permission to display their site as a portfolio example, (3) a brief 10-minute video testimonial (optional but requested), and (4) agreement to refer at least one other business owner. The agreement is required — no exceptions. This is a business exchange, not charity.

What Free Clients Get

  • Everything in the $1,999 package
  • No charge, permanently
  • Same quality, same process, same handoff
  • Priority support during build

What Sagemind Gets

  • Written testimonial (usable in marketing)
  • Portfolio permission
  • Video testimonial (if willing)
  • At least one referral ask
  • Process validation + real-world testing

Transition to Paid

Once 3–5 free sites are live and testimonials are in hand, all new clients pay full price. The free program is time-limited and capacity-limited — the written agreement makes that clear. There is no public "free tier" that persists; the offer is made to selected prospects during Month 1 only.

After the free cohort, the portfolio speaks for itself: "Here are five local businesses in the Bay Area who are already live on sites we built." That's the close.

11 Technical Architecture

Stack

  • Framework: Next.js (App Router)
  • Styling: Tailwind CSS
  • Language: TypeScript
  • Hosting: Vercel (client's account, free tier)
  • Chatbot: Client's own API key (any provider)
  • Forms: Email forwarding via Resend or similar

Config-Driven Architecture

  • Single shared codebase for all 15 verticals
  • All business-specific content in one config file
  • Updating a site = updating a TypeScript object
  • New verticals added in hours, not days
  • No CMS required — reduces complexity and cost

Dependency on AI chatbot providers Low Low Client owns the API key. If they want to switch providers, they swap the key and we can provide a 1-hr paid update. No lock-in.
