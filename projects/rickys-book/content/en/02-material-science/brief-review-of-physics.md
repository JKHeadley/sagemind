# Brief Review of Physics



Basic physics can be broken down into several key concepts, which help explain how matter and energy behave in the universe. Here’s an overview of the fundamentals:

1. Mechanics

	•	Kinematics: Describes the motion of objects without considering the causes of the motion. It includes concepts such as:

	•	Position: Where an object is located.

	•	Velocity: The speed of an object in a specific direction.

	•	Acceleration: How velocity changes over time.

	•	Dynamics: Focuses on the forces that cause motion, described by Newton’s Laws:

	•	First Law (Inertia): An object will remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted on by a force.

	•	Second Law (F=ma): The force acting on an object is equal to its mass times its acceleration.

	•	Third Law (Action-Reaction): For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

	•	Work and Energy: Work is done when a force moves an object. Energy is the ability to do work, and it can take various forms:

	•	Kinetic Energy: Energy of motion.

	•	Potential Energy: Stored energy due to position or configuration.

	•	Conservation of Energy: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.



2. Thermodynamics

	•	Heat: A form of energy transfer between systems or objects due to temperature differences.

	•	Laws of Thermodynamics:

	•	First Law: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred or converted.

	•	Second Law: The entropy (disorder) of an isolated system tends to increase.

	•	Third Law: As temperature approaches absolute zero, the entropy of a system approaches a minimum.

	•	Temperature: A measure of the average kinetic energy of particles in a system.



3. Waves and Oscillations

	•	Wave Properties: Waves transfer energy without transferring matter and can be classified as:

	•	Mechanical Waves (e.g., sound waves) require a medium to travel through.

	•	Electromagnetic Waves (e.g., light, radio waves) can travel through a vacuum.

	•	Frequency: The number of wave cycles that pass a given point per second.

	•	Amplitude: The height of the wave, related to its energy.

	•	Speed: How fast the wave travels through a medium.

	•	Reflection, Refraction, Diffraction: How waves interact with objects and boundaries.



4. Electromagnetism

	•	Electricity: The study of electric charges, currents, and circuits.

	•	Coulomb’s Law: Describes the force between two electric charges.

	•	Ohm’s Law: Relates voltage, current, and resistance in electrical circuits (V = IR).

	•	Magnetism: The study of magnetic fields and their effects on materials.

	•	Electromagnetic Spectrum: The range of all types of electromagnetic radiation, including visible light, radio waves, X-rays, etc.

	•	Maxwell’s Equations: The four fundamental equations describing how electric and magnetic fields interact.



5. Modern Physics

	•	Special Relativity: Describes the behavior of objects moving at high speeds, particularly close to the speed of light.

	•	E=mc²: Mass and energy are interchangeable.

	•	Time Dilation: Time moves slower for objects moving at high speeds.

	•	Quantum Mechanics: The study of particles at the smallest scales, where energy is quantized and particles exhibit wave-like properties.

	•	Uncertainty Principle: It’s impossible to simultaneously know both the position and velocity of a particle with perfect accuracy.

	•	Wave-Particle Duality: Particles like electrons exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties.



6. Nuclear Physics

	•	Nucleus: The center of an atom, made up of protons and neutrons.

	•	Radioactive Decay: The process by which unstable nuclei lose energy by emitting radiation.

	•	Nuclear Reactions: Includes fission (splitting atoms) and fusion (combining atoms), both of which release vast amounts of energy.

Each of these areas forms a foundation for understanding the universe, from the motion of planets to the behavior of subatomic particles.
