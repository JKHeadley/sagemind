V. Interpretation in Later Traditions

	•	Rabbinic Judaism viewed Pharisees positively and others (especially Sadducees) critically.

	•	Christianity inherited many apocalyptic, ethical, and symbolic themes from Essenes and Pharisees, but often polemicized them in scripture (e.g. Gospel critiques of Pharisees).

	•	Modern scholarship sees this period as one of incredible diversity, not a single Judaism but many “Judaisms.”
