# Review of Solomonic Inspired Initiations Preserved within York Rite Freemasonry (Royal Arch Masonry: Chapter Degrees)



Royal Arch Masonry builds upon the Master Mason degree by revealing the long-lost Word of Masonry and deepening the symbolic journey of a Mason.



# 1. Mark Master (4°)Theme: Recognition of labor and moral integrity.
# Ritual & Ceremony:# 

The candidate is symbolically received as a stonemason at King Solomon’s Temple. During the ceremony, they choose a specific mark (symbol) to represent their work. This mark is recorded, signifying their individual contributions. The candidate is then tested by overseers, learning that only work of quality and honesty is recognized.

Key Events:

- Introduction to the concept of a Mason’s “Mark.”
- The presentation of a rejected keystone, which later proves essential.
- The lesson that one’s work is judged by its quality, not outward appearances.



Symbols & Tools:

- Keystone: Represents the essential but often overlooked element in construction, symbolic of Masonry’s moral lessons.
- Mark Token: Given to the candidate, symbolizing their personal contribution to the Craft.
- Chisel & Mallet: Represent refinement and discipline.



Moral Lesson:

- Honest work and virtue are recognized in due time.
- Every Mason has a unique role in the great building of life.

# 2. Past Master (Virtual) (5°)Theme: Leadership and responsibility.
# Ritual & Ceremony:# 

This degree was originally conferred only upon actual Past Masters of a Blue Lodge, but in modern times, it is given to all Royal Arch candidates. The candidate is placed in the symbolic role of a Worshipful Master and must demonstrate leadership and decision-making.

Key Events:

- The candidate is ceremonially seated in the East (Master’s chair).
- They face symbolic trials and must make just decisions.
- They are taught humility and responsibility.

Symbols and Tools:

- The Square & Compasses (opened at a right angle): Represents the balance of wisdom and morality in leadership.
- Trestle Board: The blueprint of leadership and responsibility.



Moral Lesson:

- Leadership requires wisdom, patience, and humility.



# 3. Most Excellent Master (6°)Theme: The completion and dedication of Solomon’s Temple.
# Ritual & Ceremony:# 

The candidate symbolically participates in the dedication of Solomon’s Temple, witnessing the celebration and divine presence.

Key Events:

- A symbolic procession where the Temple is dedicated.
- The Ark of the Covenant is placed in the Holy of Holies.
- Divine approval is given, demonstrating the reward of labor and faithfulness.

Symbols:

- Ark of the Covenant: Represents the presence of God and the fulfillment of a divine mission.
- Circle of Companions: Represents unity and shared purpose.



Moral Lesson:

- Diligent work is rewarded with divine approval.



# 4. Royal Arch Mason (7°)Theme: Discovery of the Lost Word.
# Ritual & Ceremony:# 

This is the climax of Royal Arch Masonry, where the candidate, now cast as a returning exile from Babylon, assists in rebuilding the Temple and accidentally uncovers the secret vault containing the Lost Word.

Key Events:

- The candidate participates in the excavation of the ruins of the First Temple.
- They uncover a hidden vault and find a sacred treasure.
- The long-lost Word of God is revealed.

Symbols:

- Triple Tau: Represents the divine name and hidden knowledge.
- Veils of the Temple: Symbolize different levels of spiritual enlightenment.



Moral Lesson:

- True wisdom requires persistence and divine guidance.
