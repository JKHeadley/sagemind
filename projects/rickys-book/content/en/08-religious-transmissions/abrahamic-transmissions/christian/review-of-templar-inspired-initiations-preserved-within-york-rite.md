# Review of Templar Inspired Initiations Preserved within York Rite Freemasonry (Chivalric Masonry: Knights Templar Degrees)



The Chivalric Orders of the York Rite, collectively known as Chivalric Masonry, represent the culmination of the York Rite journey. These degrees shift from the symbolic, speculative Masonry of the Royal Arch and Cryptic to an overtly Christian, knightly, and militant system. The degrees here are orders rather than traditional Masonic degrees, emphasizing the ideals of medieval knighthood, Christian virtue, and spiritual warfare.



These orders draw heavily from historical chivalric traditions, particularly the Knights Templar and Knights of Malta, emphasizing service, sacrifice, and unwavering faith.



STRUCTURE OF CHIVALRIC MASONRY

Chivalric Masonry consists of three primary orders, each with a distinct historical and spiritual theme:

1.	Order of the Red Cross – A bridge between Old Testament Masonry and Christian Knighthood, focusing on Truth.

2.	Order of the Knights of Malta (Order of St. Paul) – A preparatory degree that teaches service and Christian commitment.

3.	Order of the Temple (Knights Templar Proper) – The most solemn and dramatic order, representing full commitment to the Christian faith.







# ORDER OF THE RED CROSSTheme: The supremacy of Truth over worldly power.


This order serves as a transition from the Old Testament focus of the Royal Arch to the Christian chivalry of the later orders. It draws from the Book of Ezra, where Zerubbabel, a Jewish prince, secures the decree to rebuild the Second Temple.



# Ritual & Ceremony:# 

The candidate assumes the role of Zerubbabel, one of the Jewish exiles returning from Babylon. He petitions King Darius for permission to rebuild the Temple in Jerusalem. Before granting this request, the King sets up a debate, asking:



“What is the strongest force in the world?”


Three answers are given:

1.	Wine – It can influence men’s decisions and cloud judgment.

2.	The King – He has absolute authority over life and death.

3.	Women – They hold sway over men’s hearts and decisions.



Zerubbabel, however, argues that Truth is the strongest of all because it is eternal and unchanging. The King agrees and allows him to rebuild the Temple, proving that Truth triumphs over all things.



# Symbols:

- Red Cross – Represents the blood of sacrifice and the struggle for truth.
- Sword & Trowel – Symbolizes both the militant and constructive nature of the order (rebuilding the Temple while prepared for battle).
- Scroll of Decrees – Represents divine and legal authority.



# Lessons:# 

- Truth is mightier than worldly power.
- Faith in divine justice leads to victory.
- One must be willing to defend righteousness, even against kings.

# ORDER OF THE KNIGHTS OF MALTA (ORDER OF ST. PAUL)Theme: Defending Christianity and serving humanity.


The Order of Malta introduces an overt Christian chivalric identity. It draws inspiration from the Knights Hospitaller (Order of St. John), who protected Christian pilgrims and served as warrior-monks during the Crusades.



# Ritual & Ceremony:# 

The candidate is symbolically made a pilgrim who seeks admission into the Order of Malta. They are questioned about their faith, character, and willingness to serve Christ. If deemed worthy, they are admitted and take solemn vows.



# Symbols:# 

- Maltese Cross – Represents the eight beatitudes and the spiritual virtues of knighthood.
- Black Mantle with White Cross – Symbolizes humility, service, and martyrdom.
- Sword and Shield – Denote the knight’s duty to defend the faith and fight against evil.



# Lessons:

- Faith must be defended against all enemies, spiritual and temporal.
- True Christian service is found in aiding the sick, poor, and helpless.
- Chivalry demands humility, sacrifice, and unwavering commitment.

# ORDER OF THE TEMPLE (KNIGHTS TEMPLAR)Theme: A knight’s total devotion to Christ and the willingness to die for one’s faith.


This is the most solemn and dramatic order in all of Freemasonry. It represents a knight’s spiritual death, rebirth, and ultimate service to God. It is not merely an honorary title—it symbolizes a profound commitment to living as a knight of Christ.



# Ritual & Ceremony:# 

The initiation into the Knights Templar is lengthy, deeply symbolic, and highly ritualistic. The candidate is placed through multiple trials, culminating in a symbolic death and resurrection.



1. Pilgrim’s Journey:

- The candidate begins as a poor, weary pilgrim seeking admission to the Order.
- He is subjected to trials of humility, penance, and sacrifice.
- He learns that he must renounce the sins of the flesh and devote himself to God’s will.



2. The Vigil:

- The candidate undergoes a night of prayer and reflection before taking his vows, just as medieval knights did before being dubbed.
- He contemplates mortality, duty, and faith, symbolized by the Skull and Crossbones before him.



3. The Final Oath:

- The candidate is presented with a cross, a sword, and a Bible.
- He takes solemn vows to defend the Christian faith and fight against infidels and corruption.
- He pledges his life to honor, charity, and truth.



4. The Knighthood:

- The candidate is knighted with the sword of faith.
- He is clothed in the white mantle of the Templars, symbolizing purity and devotion.
- He is now a Knight of the Temple of Jerusalem, sworn to serve Christ until death.



# Symbols:

- Red Cross Pattee – The Templar cross, symbolizing sacrifice and martyrdom.
- Skull and Crossbones – Represents mortality and the need to prepare for death in righteousness.
- Sword of the Spirit – Symbolizes both literal and spiritual combat against evil.
- Pilgrim’s Staffand Scallop – Represents the journey of faith and the trials of a knight’s life alluding to the Templar’s pilgrimage route to the shrine of St. James the Great in Santiago de Compostela, Spain 



# Lessons:# 

- A true knight must be ready to give his life for his faith.
- Honor, truth, and sacrifice are the highest virtues.
- Christianity must be defended from corruption, heresy, and enemies.

Thus, the Knight Templar degree is an internal transformation of the soul, in which the Mason dies to his lower nature and is reborn as a spiritual warrior.



2. The Skull and Crossbones: Memento Mori & Initiatic Death

During the Vigil of the Knights Templar initiation, the candidate is placed before a skull and crossbones, reminding him of his mortality.

- Esoteric Meaning: This symbolizes Memento Mori (“Remember that you must die”), reinforcing the idea that the knight must live righteously, for death is inevitable.
- Initiatic Death: The candidate experiences a symbolic death before being reborn as a true knight. This echoes ancient mystery school initiations, where one had to “die” before receiving divine wisdom.

The use of the skull links the Templar initiation to Christian esoteric traditions, alchemical symbolism, and even medieval death cults like the Danse Macabre.



3. The Red Cross & The Crusader’s Mission

The Red Cross Pattee worn by Templars is not just a symbol of martyrdom but also a sign of the “inner crusade”—the fight against ignorance, sin, and corruption.

- The Outer Crusade → Fighting to reclaim the Holy Land.
- The Inner Crusade → Fighting for virtue, truth, and divine knowledge within oneself.

The Masonic Knight does not wield a sword in war but in spiritual battle—against falsehood, injustice, and personal weakness.



4. The Sword as the Word of God

The sword given to the new Templar is not merely a weapon—it is the Sword of the Spirit, representing the Word of God (Ephesians 6:17).

- In historical chivalry: The sword was the knight’s ultimate tool of defense and justice.
- In spiritual symbolism: The sword represents divine truth, wisdom, and the power of Christ’s teachings.

The Masonic Knight swears to use his sword only in defense of the faith, justice, and the oppressed, echoing the medieval Templar ethos.



5. The White Mantle and the Path of Purity

When the candidate is knighted, he receives a white mantle with a red cross.

- White = Purity & Devotion to Christ.
- Red Cross = Willingness to shed blood in the cause of righteousness.

Historically, the white mantle of the original Knights Templar symbolized their vow of chastity and their monastic warrior status—living as monks and fighting as knights.

- In Masonic symbolism: The white mantle is an outward sign of inward purity. The knight must live a life free from moral corruption and uphold the virtues of faith, hope, and charity.

While Masonic Knights Templar are not direct descendants of the medieval Knights Templar, they embody many of the same ideals.
