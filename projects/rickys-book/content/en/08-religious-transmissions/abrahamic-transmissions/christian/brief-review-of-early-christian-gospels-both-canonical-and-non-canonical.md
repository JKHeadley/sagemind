# Brief Review of Early Christian Gospels both Canonical and Non-Canonical



|
Gospel Name
 |
Primary Audience
 |
Key Features
 |
Major Themes
 |  |
|
Mark (canonical)
 |
Roman or persecuted Gentile Christians
 |
Shortest; urgent tone; action-driven; few teachings; abrupt ending
 |
Suffering Messiah, secrecy motif (Messianic secret), discipleship through hardship
 |  |
|
Matthew (canonical)
 |
Jewish Christians
 |
Structured like Torah (5 discourses); heavy OT quotes; emphasizes prophecy
 |
Jesus as New Moses, fulfillment of prophecy, Kingdom ethics
 |  |
|
Luke (canonical)
 |
Hellenistic Gentile Christians, marginalized
 |
Longest gospel; polished Greek; universal tone; women, poor, outcasts
 |
Universal salvation, compassion, Holy Spirit, history of the Church
 |  |
|
John (canonical)
 |
Hellenized Jewish and Gentile Christians
 |
Poetic, symbolic; not synoptic; signs and “I AM” sayings; cosmic prologue
 |
Divinity of Jesus, eternal life, dualism (light/dark), love
 |  |
|
Gospel of Thomas
 |
Mystical/gnostic-leaning Christians
 |
114 sayings of Jesus; no narrative; secret knowledge
 |
Inner enlightenment, self-knowledge, kingdom within
 |  |
|
Gospel of Mary
 |
Gnostic Christians; possibly women leaders
 |
Fragmentary; dialogue after resurrection; Mary as teacher
 |
Spiritual resurrection, inner truth, critique of Peter’s authority
 |  |
|
Gospel of Peter
 |
Apocalyptic Christians (possibly anti-Jewish)
 |
Passion narrative; dramatic resurrection scene; Jesus doesn’t suffer
 |
Triumph over death, cosmic conflict, anti-Judaic tone
 |  |
|
Gospel of Judas
 |
Sethian Gnostic sect
 |
Dialogue between Jesus and Judas; Judas as enlightened
 |
Secret cosmology, critique of church leaders, betrayal as obedience
 |  |
|
Infancy Gospel of James
 |
Early Marian devotees
 |
Focuses on Mary’s miraculous birth and childhood of Jesus
 |
Purity, divine intervention, virginity of Mary
 |  |
|
Gospel of the Hebrews
 |
Jewish-Christian sect (e.g., Ebionites)
 |
Known only via fragments/quotes by Church Fathers
 |
Possibly emphasized Jesus’ humanity and obedience to Law
 |  |
