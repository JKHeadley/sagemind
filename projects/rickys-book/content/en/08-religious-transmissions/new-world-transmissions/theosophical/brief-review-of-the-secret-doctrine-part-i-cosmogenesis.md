# Brief Review of The Secret Doctrine (Part I: Cosmogenesis)



I. STRUCTURE OF COSMOGENESIS

Blavatsky begins *The Secret Doctrine* with a *Proem*, followed by the explanation of Three Fundamental Propositions, and then proceeds to expound upon the Seven Stanzas from the Book of Dzyan, an allegedly ancient Tibetan scripture known only to initiates. Each stanza is interpreted in light of esoteric science, metaphysics, and comparative religion.



II. THE THREE FUNDAMENTAL PROPOSITIONS

	1.	The One Absolute Reality

	•	An omnipresent, eternal, boundless, and immutable Principle beyond all human speculation.

	•	Called the *Unmanifested Logos*, *Parabrahman* (Hinduism), *Ain Soph* (Kabbalah), or *Space*.

	•	It is neither Being nor non-Being — the *Sat* and *Asat*. It precedes all dualities.

	2.	The Law of Periodicity

	•	The universe is an eternal *breathing in and out* (like Brahma’s day and night).

	•	Cycles of manifestation and dissolution: *manvantaras* (active periods) and *pralayas* (rest periods).

	•	Everything manifests, evolves, dissolves, and re-emerges in cosmic rhythm.

	3.	The Unity of All Souls with the Oversoul

	•	All individual consciousnesses (*monads*) are sparks from the one flame.

	•	They evolve through countless reincarnations across worlds and forms (metempsychosis).

	•	This unity underlies the Law of Karma and Dharma.

III. SEVEN STANZAS FROM THE BOOK OF DZYAN

These stanzas poetically express the birth and development of the cosmos from the Absolute. Below is a breakdown of each stanza with interpretation:

STANZA I – The Night of the Universe

“The eternal Parent, wrapped in her ever-invisible robes, had slumbered once again for seven eternities.”

	•	Symbolism: Pre-cosmic state; pure potential.

	•	Key Concepts:

	•	Absolute Space is both mother (matter) and father (spirit).

	•	“Seven eternities” refers to symbolic aeons — not literal time.

	•	No differentiation yet between spirit and matter.



STANZA II – The Idea of Differentiation Arises

“Where were the builders, the luminous sons of manvantaric dawn?”

	•	Symbolism: The first stirring of consciousness.

	•	Key Concepts:

	•	The emergence of primordial thought (*Mahat*) from undifferentiated space.

	•	Cosmic builders (Dhyan Chohans) arise — analogous to Archangels or emanations in Gnostic thought.

	•	The archetypal *Cosmic Egg* begins to form.



STANZA III – The Awakening of the Cosmos

“The hour had not yet struck; the ray had not yet flashed into the germ.”

	•	Symbolism: Transition from potential to active motion.

	•	Key Concepts:

	•	The “ray” is the first Logos — the light of divine consciousness.

	•	Germ of the universe is fertilized by divine ideation.

	•	Akasha (subtle cosmic matter) begins to be infused with motion.



STANZA IV – The Beginning of Cosmic Evolution

“The Seven were not yet born. Darkness alone filled the boundless all.”

	•	Symbolism: The Logos divides into aspects.

	•	Key Concepts:

	•	Emergence of the *Three Logoi*:

	1.	First Logos: Unmanifested source

	2.	Second Logos: Universal Mind (Mahat)

	3.	Third Logos: Creative force in nature

	•	Birth of *Fohat*, the dynamic force that connects spirit and matter.

	•	Differentiation into seven rays, seven planes, and seven principles.



STANZA V – Fohat Begins His Work

“Fohat traces spiral lines to unite the sixth to the seventh — the heart of the universe.”

	•	Symbolism: Fohat as divine electricity shaping the cosmos.

	•	Key Concepts:

	•	Fohat is analogous to the “Word” or “Logos” in other systems.

	•	He causes atoms to aggregate, form systems, and evolve complexity.

	•	The cosmos develops according to sacred geometry (spirals, vortices).



STANZA VI – Formation of the Planes of Existence

“From the effulgency of light — the ray of the ever-darkness — sprang in space the re-awakened energies.”

	•	Symbolism: Structuring of the universe into planes and forms.

	•	Key Concepts:

	•	Seven cosmic planes arise — from divine spirit to gross matter.

	•	Seven principles correspond to human anatomy, the planets, and the chakras.

	•	The planetary chain (like Earth’s evolution) emerges as one of many such systems.



STANZA VII – The Evolution of Consciousness

“Behold the beginning of sentient life.”

	•	Symbolism: The stage is now set for human evolution.

	•	Key Concepts:

	•	The monads begin their pilgrimage through kingdoms of nature (mineral → plant → animal → human → divine).

	•	Human beings are not created but evolve gradually through rounds and races.

	•	This stanza sets the foundation for Volume II (*Anthropogenesis*).



IV. KEY CONCEPTS IN COSMOGENESIS

	1.	Three Logoi

	•	First Logos: Unmanifested (Parabrahman)

	•	Second Logos: Universal Mind (*Mahat*)

	•	Third Logos: Creative Logos (*Fohat*)

	2.	Seven Principles of the Universe (and of Man)

	1.	Atman (Spirit)

	2.	Buddhi (Soul)

	3.	Manas (Mind)

	4.	Kama (Desire)

	5.	Prana (Vital Energy)

	6.	Linga Sharira (Astral Body)

	7.	Sthula Sharira (Physical Body)

	3.	Seven Planes of Existence

	•	Correspond to levels of reality from pure spirit to dense matter.

	•	Include spiritual, mental, astral, and physical dimensions.

	4.	Cycles and Chains

	•	Each planet goes through *Seven Rounds*, and each Round contains *Seven Root Races*.

	•	Our Earth is the fourth globe in the fourth round of its planetary chain.

	5.	Fohat

	•	Cosmic electricity, the agent of differentiation.

	•	Connects mind (Logos) and matter (Mulaprakriti).

	6.	Karma and Evolution

	•	Karma governs the movement of monads through forms.

	•	Evolution is both physical and spiritual, culminating in return to unity.

VI. INTERPRETATION & INFLUENCE

Blavatsky’s cosmology is not literal but symbolic, metaphysical, and initiatory. It influenced:

	•	The Theosophical Society and its successors

	•	Modern esotericism (Alice Bailey, Steiner, etc.)

	•	Jungian archetypes (especially the evolution of consciousness)

	•	Eastern-Western mystical synthesis

	•	Occult orders and mystical societies in the 20th century
