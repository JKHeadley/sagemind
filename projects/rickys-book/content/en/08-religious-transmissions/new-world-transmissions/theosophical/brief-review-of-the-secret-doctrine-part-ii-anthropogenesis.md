# Brief Review of The Secret Doctrine (Part II: Anthropogenesis)



I. The Fundamental Framework of Anthropogenesis

Blavatsky presents an alternative evolutionary model that integrates cosmic, spiritual, and physical evolution into a single process. Unlike Darwinism, which focuses on biological adaptation, her model is fundamentally emanationist and cyclical, drawing from Hindu, Buddhist, and Gnostic traditions.



Key Tenets:

	1.	Emanation and Descent into Matter

	•	Humanity originates from a divine, spiritual source and gradually descends into material existence.

	•	Each stage of descent corresponds to increasing density and separation from pure spirit.

	2.	Cycles of Evolution (Rounds and Globes)

	•	The Earth is not a singular entity but part of a larger cosmic cycle called a “Planetary Chain.”

	•	Humanity evolves across seven “Rounds”, where the Earth changes form and hosts different kinds of beings.

	•	Each Round consists of seven Globes (A to G), which represent different states of existence—some more spiritual, others more material.

	3.	Dual Evolution: Spiritual & Physical

	•	Evolution is not merely biological but also spiritual, with the soul evolving independently of the body.

	•	Early human forms were more ethereal and only gradually became physical.

	4.	Rejection of Darwinian Evolution

	•	Blavatsky sees Darwinism as a partial truth: while species adapt, they do not arise from random mutations but through preordained spiritual cycles.

	•	She argues that apes are actually the descendants of early degenerate human forms, rather than human ancestors.



II. The Seven Root Races

The most controversial and esoteric aspect of *Anthropogenesis* is its description of the Seven Root Races—each representing a stage in the evolution of consciousness and form.



1. The First Root Race – The Polarian Race

	•	Nature: Non-physical, composed of etheric energy rather than solid matter. These beings were formless and luminous, more like astral projections than actual biological entities.

	•	Reproduction: Asexual, through a process of division or budding, similar to cell mitosis but on a spiritual level.

	•	Consciousness: Completely unified with the divine. They had no sense of individuality, existing in a state of pure spiritual awareness.

	•	Location: Their domain was the North Pole, but not in its current physical form. Blavatsky describes it as an etheric continent, existing on a subtler plane.

	•	End of the Race: This race did not “die” but evolved into the next stage by gradually densifying and becoming more material.



2. The Second Root Race – The Hyperboreans

	•	Nature: Semi-physical, still luminous but beginning to take shape. Described as gelatinous or vaporous beings.

	•	Reproduction: Similar to the First Race, but now through external budding—a single entity could produce smaller versions of itself.

	•	Consciousness: More individualized but still highly connected to divine consciousness.

	•	Location: Lived in Hyperborea, a vast, now-lost continent that extended over what is now the Arctic and northern Eurasia.

	•	End of the Race: As these beings became denser, they slowly transformed into the next Root Race.



3. The Third Root Race – The Lemurians

	•	Nature: Fully physical, but enormous in stature, sometimes described as gigantic beings, many meters tall.

	•	Reproduction: Initially hermaphroditic, later transitioning into sexual reproduction, marking a crucial point in human evolution.

	•	Consciousness: Developed individual self-awareness, but still retained a strong connection to spiritual forces.

	•	Notable Features:

	•	Third Eye: Early Lemurians had a spiritual organ (later atrophied into the pineal gland) that allowed them to see into higher dimensions.

	•	Creation of the First Civilizations: They built massive stone structures (which Blavatsky suggests are the remnants of ancient monolithic sites).

	•	Origin of Animals: According to Blavatsky, some Lemurians engaged in forbidden interbreeding with animals, resulting in the creation of apes and other lower creatures.

	•	Location: The continent of Lemuria, which Blavatsky believed existed in the Pacific and Indian Oceans, connecting Madagascar, Australia, and South America.

	•	End of the Race: Lemuria was destroyed by a series of volcanic cataclysms due to its inhabitants’ increasing materialism and misuse of spiritual knowledge.



4. The Fourth Root Race – The Atlanteans

	•	Nature: Physically similar to modern humans but still larger and stronger, with some retaining psychic abilities.

	•	Consciousness:

	•	The early Atlanteans were deeply spiritual and wise, having advanced sciences and civilizations.

	•	The later Atlanteans became highly materialistic and corrupt, leading to their downfall.

	•	Achievements:

	•	Built vast cities with advanced technology.

	•	Possessed powerful psychic abilities, including telepathy and telekinesis.

	•	Developed flying machines (Vimanas) and a form of energy manipulation.

	•	Corruption and Downfall:

	•	Engaged in black magic and genetic experimentation.

	•	Created hybrid beings, which contributed to chaos and suffering.

	•	This misuse of knowledge led to a great flood, sinking Atlantis beneath the ocean.

	•	Survivors: Some Atlanteans fled to Egypt, the Americas, and other ancient lands, influencing early civilizations.



5. The Fifth Root Race – The Aryans (Current Humanity)

	•	Nature: Our present race, still evolving spiritually and intellectually.

	•	Consciousness:

	•	More intellectual than psychic, with a focus on material development and rationality.

	•	Still far from full spiritual enlightenment.

	•	Mission: To bridge the gap between material and spiritual understanding.

	•	Location: Spread across Europe, Asia, and the Americas.

	•	Challenges:

	•	Struggles with materialism, warfare, and loss of spiritual insight.

	•	Moving toward a new cycle of consciousness, preparing for the Sixth Root Race.



6. The Sixth Root Race (Future Humanity)

	•	Nature: More spiritually evolved, moving beyond physical limitations.

	•	Consciousness:

	•	Highly intuitive, psychic, and in harmony with nature.

	•	Will develop new faculties of perception, such as telepathy.

	•	Location: Expected to arise in North America, parts of Asia, and new lands that will emerge after Earth changes.

	•	Mission: To unify science and spirituality, restoring Atlantean wisdom without its corruption.



7. The Seventh Root Race (Final Humanity)

	•	Nature: No longer confined to physical existence, transitioning into pure energy beings.

	•	Consciousness:

	•	Achieves total spiritual enlightenment and reunites with the divine.

	•	Ends the cycle of physical incarnation.

	•	Mission: Completes Earth’s purpose before the next cosmic cycle begins.

	•	Location: A transformed, purified Earth or another plane of existence.



III. The Esoteric Structure of Evolution

Blavatsky integrates several mystical models into her anthropological framework.

1. The Seven Rounds

	•	Humanity does not evolve in a single linear path but through seven great evolutionary cycles (Rounds).

	•	We are currently in the Fourth Round, which is the most material phase.

	•	In future Rounds, we will become increasingly spiritual again.



2. The Chain of Globes

	•	The Earth is one of seven interlinked planets forming a Planetary Chain.

	•	Humans have lived on other planets in different forms before incarnating here.



3. Karma and Reincarnation

	•	The human soul reincarnates repeatedly to refine itself.

	•	Each lifetime is part of a grand cosmic lesson, moving toward enlightenment.



IV. The Fall of Man and the Role of the “Lords of Wisdom”

Blavatsky reinterprets the Fall of Man as a necessary phase of evolution.

	•	The “Lords of Wisdom” (Manasaputras) were spiritual beings who bestowed self-awareness upon early humans.

	•	This act is similar to Prometheus stealing fire bringing knowledge.

	•	However, some beings misused their knowledge, leading to material corruption (Atlantis’ downfall).



V. Influence and Controversy

1. Influence on Occultism

	•	Inspired Theosophy, Rudolf Steiner’s Anthroposophy, Alice Bailey’s teachings, and later New Age movements.

	•	Influenced esoteric interpretations of Atlantis and Lemuria.



2. Controversial Aspects

	•	Some of Blavatsky’s racial theories have been misused for racist ideologies, though she did not advocate racism in a modern sense.

	•	Her claims of ancient texts (Stanzas of Dzyan) are disputed.
