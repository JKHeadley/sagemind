# Brief Review of Theosophy



I. ORIGINS OF THEOSOPHY

1. Historical Background

	•	Founded: 1875 in New York City.

	•	Founders: Helena Petrovna Blavatsky, Henry Steel Olcott, and William Quan Judge.

	•	Institution: The Theosophical Society — intended to explore ancient wisdom, comparative religion, and latent human potential.

	•	The movement was partly a reaction to materialism, dogmatic Christianity, and Western colonial views of “Eastern” religions.



2. Philosophical and Religious Roots

	•	Neoplatonism: The doctrine of emanation, the One, and the soul’s journey.

	•	Hermeticism and Gnosticism: Emphasis on hidden knowledge and the fall/reintegration of the soul.

	•	Indian Philosophy: Especially Advaita Vedanta, Yoga, and Samkhya.

	•	Buddhism: Elements of Mahāyāna and early Tibetan ideas, adapted to fit Theosophical metaphysics.

	•	Western Occultism: Rosicrucianism, Freemasonry, and Kabbalah.



II. CORE DOCTRINES

1. The Three Declared Objects of the Theosophical Society

	1.	To form a nucleus of the universal brotherhood of humanity, without distinction of race, creed, sex, caste, or color.

	2.	To encourage the study of comparative religion, philosophy, and science.

	3.	To investigate unexplained laws of nature and the powers latent in man.

2. The Sevenfold Constitution of the Human Being

Derived from Hindu metaphysics, especially the Upaniṣads:

	1.	Sthūla-śarīra (Physical Body)

	2.	Prāṇa (Vital force)

	3.	Kāma (Desire body)

	4.	Manas (Mind — split into lower and higher aspects)

	5.	Buddhi (Spiritual intuition)

	6.	Ātman (Universal spirit)



This structure mirrors esoteric psychology: lower (mortal) and higher (immortal) principles, linked via spiritual development.



3. Karma and Reincarnation

	•	Karma: The cosmic law of moral cause and effect.

	•	Reincarnation: The soul evolves through cycles of birth and rebirth toward divine realization.

	•	Souls incarnate to learn and expiate karma, climbing the spiral ladder of spiritual evolution.



4. Cosmology and Root Races

From Blavatsky’s *The Secret Doctrine*:

	•	Humanity evolves in vast cycles through seven Root Races:

	1.	Polarian (etheric, non-physical)

	2.	Hyperborean

	3.	Lemurian

	4.	Atlantean

	5.	Aryan (the current)

	6.	Future Races: yet to come, more spiritualized beings

	•	Each Root Race passes through seven subraces. This doctrine combines myth, allegory, and evolutionary speculation.

	•	The Earth itself is one of a series of Globes in a septenary chain.



5. The Secret Doctrine of Esoteric Evolution

	•	Evolution occurs on three fronts:

	•	Spiritual evolution: descent and re-ascent of spirit through matter.

	•	Monadic evolution: the individual divine spark experiences all levels of being.

	•	Intellectual evolution: development of manas and conscious self-awareness.

	•	Influenced by Indian concepts of Māyā (illusion) and Līlā (cosmic play).



III. KEY TEXTS

	1.	Isis Unveiled (1877) – Blavatsky’s first major work; a critique of materialism and religion, advocating esoteric knowledge.

	2.	The Secret Doctrine (1888) – A cosmological and anthropological blueprint of spiritual evolution, drawing from the Stanzas of Dzyan, said to be ancient Tibetan texts.

	3.	The Key to Theosophy (1889) – A Q&A format manual explaining Theosophical beliefs.

	4.	The Voice of the Silence (1889) – A mystical guide for aspirants on the spiritual path, influenced by Mahāyāna and Vajrayāna ideals.



IV. KEY FIGURES AND BRANCHES

1. Helena P. Blavatsky

	•	Russian mystic and the central architect of Theosophical thought.

	•	Claimed contact with the *Mahatmas*, advanced spiritual beings guiding humanity.

2. Henry Steel Olcott

	•	Co-founder, organized the Society in India, deeply involved in Buddhist revival.

3. Annie Besant

	•	Socialist and women’s rights advocate turned Theosophist.

	•	Helped spread Theosophy globally, especially in India.

	•	Promoted Jiddu Krishnamurti as a potential World Teacher.



4. Charles W. Leadbeater

	•	Clairvoyant; expanded Theosophical cosmology with insights on chakras, astral bodies, and etheric forms.

	•	Co-authored *Man: Whence, How and Whither*.

5. Rudolf Steiner

	•	Initially involved in Theosophy, broke off to form Anthroposophy, with a more Christian-centric esotericism.



6. Alice Bailey

	•	Developed Arcane School and the concept of the Seven Rays, leading to modern New Age currents.



V. SYMBOLS AND ICONOGRAPHY

	•	Seal of the Theosophical Society includes:

	•	Ouroboros (eternity)

	•	Swastika (evolution and cosmic force)

	•	Ankh (life)

	•	Star of David (macrocosm/microcosm)

	•	Aum (universal vibration)



VI. MYSTICAL AND ESOTERIC PRACTICES

	•	Meditation, especially to connect with higher principles.

	•	Study of ancient scriptures from all traditions.

	•	Clairvoyance and astral travel (especially emphasized by Leadbeater).

	•	Spiritual evolution and service as initiation.

	•	Silence, ethical purification, and detachment from desire (linked to Mahāyāna ideals).



VII. INFLUENCE AND LEGACY

1. Eastern Influence in the West

	•	First major introduction of Hinduism and Buddhism to the Western world.

	•	Influenced later yoga movements, mindfulness, and New Age spirituality.



2. Esotericism and Occultism

	•	Precursor to New Age thought, Rosicrucian revival, Neo-Gnosticism, and modern Western esotericism.

	•	Influence on Carl Jung, Joseph Campbell, and Integral Theory (e.g. Ken Wilber).



3. Art and Literature

	•	Inspired Symbolist painters, poets (W.B. Yeats), and early 20th-century occult writers.

	•	Influential in the development of transpersonal psychology and alternative healing.



VIII. CRITIQUES AND CONTROVERSIES

1. Cultural Appropriation

	•	Accused of misrepresenting Eastern doctrines and appropriating Indian, Buddhist, and Tibetan traditions in Westernized forms.



2. Racial Hierarchies

	•	The Root Race doctrine has been critiqued for racial implications, despite its metaphysical intent.

	•	The notion of “Aryan” and “spiritual evolution” has been misused or misunderstood.



3. Claims of Supernatural Contact

	•	Skepticism about Blavatsky’s *Mahatmas* and channeled texts.

	•	Some scholars (like Hodgson in the SPR report) accused Blavatsky of fraud — although later evidence questioned those conclusions.

4. Schisms and Splits

	•	Major divisions in the Society, including Steiner’s Anthroposophy, Bailey’s Arcane School, and Judge’s American branch.

IX. THEOSOPHY TODAY

	•	Survives in the Theosophical Society Adyar, The Theosophical Society Pasadena, and United Lodge of Theosophists.

	•	Influences modern yoga, esotericism, mysticism, and spiritual pluralism.

	•	Integral to the development of Western esotericism as an academic field.
