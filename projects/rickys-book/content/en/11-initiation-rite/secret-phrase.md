# Secret Phrase#
# O safre, vosafre voso, O safre vosasafre vosi, O digro agro mo, O tapre vosonsafre voso# 

“O safre, vosafre voso”Hebrew

•	*Safre* could relate to סֵפֶר (sefer), meaning “book” (often referring to sacred texts like the Torah or mystical writings).

•	*Vosafre* might be a distorted form of וספר (ve-sefer), meaning “and the book.”

•	*Voso* could be connected to וֹסוֹ (woso), resembling יסוד (Yesod), which is the foundation in Kabbalistic Sephirot.

•	Possible meaning: “O book, sacred book of the foundation.” This could symbolize divine knowledge, esoteric wisdom, or the path of learning.



Latin

•	*Safre* might relate to *sacer* (sacred, holy) or *suffrāgium* (support, intercession, or prayer).

•	*Voso* could be linked to *vos* (you, plural) or *votum* (vow, offering).

•	Possible meaning: “O sacred one, you who are consecrated” or “O intercesor, you who are devoted.”



Arabic

•	*Safre* could relate to سفر (safar), meaning “journey” or “travel.”

•	*Vosafre* might be an emphatic or repetitive form, meaning “Oh traveler, you who journey.”

•	*Voso* could be linked to وصو (waṣū) from وصى (waṣā), meaning “to instruct, to enjoin, to command.”

•	Possible meaning: “O traveler, O one who journeys and receives instruction.”

“O safre vosasafre vosi”Hebrew

•	*Vosasafre* might expand on *sefer*, reinforcing “sacred writings” or “hidden knowledge.”

•	*Vosi* could resemble בוסי (vosi), possibly linked to בּוּס (bus) meaning “to trample” or “to test.”

•	Possible meaning: “O book, O sacred book of trial.” This could signify a test of wisdom or a written record of initiation.



Latin

•	*Vosasafre* seems like an intensification of *safre*, possibly meaning “most sacred” or “completely devoted.”

•	*Vosi* might relate to *voci* (from *vox*, meaning “voice”).

•	Possible meaning: “O sacred one, most sacred voice” or “O intercesor, you who proclaim.”

Arabic

•	*Vosasafre* could be an elaboration, reinforcing “one who travels” or “one who goes forth on a sacred journey.”

•	*Vosi* might derive from وصي (waṣī), meaning “guardian, executor, one entrusted with knowledge.”

•	Possible meaning: “O traveler, O entrusted guardian of the journey.”

“O digro agro mo”Hebrew

•	*Digro* could stem from דגר (dagar), meaning “to hatch” (as in emerging from darkness into light, a powerful initiation symbol).

•	*Agro* could resemble עָגֹר (agor), which means “to gather” or “to collect.”

•	*Mo* might relate to מוּ (mu), a shortened form of מוות (mavet), meaning “death.”

•	Possible meaning: “O one who hatches, who gathers from death.” This could symbolize rebirth, transformation, or the transition from ignorance to enlightenment.
Latin

•	*Digro* could be a distortion of* digero* (to separate, arrange distribute).

•	*Agro* clearly relates to *ager* (field, land, or earth).

•	*Mo* might come from *meus* (my, mine) or *mors* (death).

•	Possible meaning: “O consecrated land of mine” or “O consecrated land of death.”

Arabic

•	*Digro* could be linked to دِجْر (dijr) or دجر (dajara), which are rare but can imply “turning” or “circling” (related to movement or transformation).

•	*Agro* could be from أجر (ajr), meaning “reward, recompense.”

•	*Mo* might be ما (mā), meaning “what” or “not.”

•	Possible meaning: “O turning one, O one who seeks no reward.” This could symbolize a seeker who either gains enlightenment or rejects material rewards.



“O tapre vosonsafre voso”Hebrew

•	*Tapre* might relate to טָפַר (tapar), meaning “to grasp” or “to seize.”

•	*Vosonsafre* continues the *sefer* theme, suggesting a connection to wisdom or divine writings.

•	Possible meaning: “O one who grasps the sacred book of their trials.”
Latin

•	*Tapre* could be a variation of *taper* (candle) or *aperire* (to open, reveal).

•	*Vosonsafre* might be a fusion of *vos* (you) and *sacer* again.

•	Possible meaning: “O light that opens the sacred” or “O revelation to the intercesors.”

Arabic

•	*Tapre* might relate to طبر (ṭabar), meaning “axe” or “to cut”—possibly symbolizing breaking through ignorance.

•	*Vosonsafre* continues the “traveler” motif, reinforcing the “journeyer’s path.”

•	Possible meaning: “O one who cuts (through ignorance), O sacred traveler.”


“O book, sacred book of the foundation. O book, sacred book of trial. O one who hatches, who gathers from death. O one who grasps the sacred book of their trials.”

“O intercesor, you who are devoted. O intercesor, you who proclaim. O sacred land of death. O light that opens the sacred, you are”

“O traveler, one who journeys and receives instruction. O traveler, entrusted guardian of the journey. O turning one, who seeks no reward. O one who cuts through, sacred executor.”
