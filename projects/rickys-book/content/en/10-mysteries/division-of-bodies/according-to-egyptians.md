# According to Egpytians



1. Khat — Physical Body

	•	Meaning: The corpse, physical form; the tangible body that decays after death.

	•	Function: The vessel that houses the other parts during life; preserved through mummification.



2. Ka — Vital Essence / Double

	•	Meaning: The life-force, double, or vital essence; the energetic twin that survives death.

	•	Function: Closely tied to sustenance, offerings, and ancestral presence. It needed food and rituals after death.



3. Ba — Personality / Soul

	•	Meaning: The individual soul, personality, or consciousness; capable of mobility between realms.

	•	Depiction: Often shown as a human-headed bird, able to leave the tomb and visit the living.



4. Ib (jb / ỉb) — Heart / Seat of Consciousness

	•	Meaning: The heart, thought to be the seat of emotion, thought, memory, and morality.

	•	Function: Weighed against Ma’at (truth/order) in the Hall of Judgment.

	•	Root: Possibly from the hieroglyphic determinative for “heart and vessels.”



5. Sheut (šwt) — Shadow

	•	Meaning: The shadow or double presence of the person; considered semi-autonomous and tied to identity.

	•	Function: Omnipresent and part of the person’s essence; could act independently in some magical texts.



6. Ren (rn) — True Name

	•	Meaning: The true name of the person, essential for their continued existence.

	•	Function: To speak or write one’s name was to preserve them; to erase it was to annihilate them from memory and the afterlife.

7. Akh — Transfigured Spirit / Luminous Being

	•	Meaning: The spirit that survives death and becomes an immortal being in the afterlife.

	•	Root: Related to the word for “light” or “to be effective.”

	•	Function: A successful transformation of the *ba* and *ka* into a luminous, blessed form.
