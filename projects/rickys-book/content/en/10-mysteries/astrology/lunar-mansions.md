# Lunar Mansions



I. Islamic Lunar Mansions (Manāzil al-Qamar / منزل القمر)

Overview

	•	The Islamic lunar mansion system comprises 28 segments of the Moon’s monthly path (sidereal month).

	•	Each mansion roughly corresponds to the Moon’s position for one day in its 27.3-day cycle.

	•	Derived from older Babylonian and Indian systems, adapted by Arab Bedouins and integrated into Islamic astronomy.

	•	Used for astrology, calendrical calculations, navigation, agriculture, and weather prediction.

Structure

	•	Called “Manāzil al-Qamar” (Stations of the Moon).

	•	Each mansion corresponds to a star or star group along the Moon’s apparent path.

	•	Each has a name, usually linked to a star asterism, and symbolic meanings often connected to weather patterns, omens, or tribal lore.



Functions

	•	Agricultural Calendars: Tied to the sowing and harvesting seasons.

	•	Weather Prediction: Each mansion had associated seasonal weather phenomena.

	•	Astrology & Magic: Used in talismanic magic, similar to Indian and Persian traditions.

	•	Navigation: Bedouins used them to orient themselves in the desert.



II. Chinese Lunar Mansions (二十八宿, Èrshíbā Xiù)

Overview

	•	Also based on the sidereal lunar cycle, the Chinese system has 28 lunar lodges.

	•	Part of the Chinese celestial bureaucracy, linking astronomy with mythology, cosmology, medicine, and feng shui.

	•	The 28 mansions are divided into 4 cardinal groups, each linked to a mythological beast and a direction.
