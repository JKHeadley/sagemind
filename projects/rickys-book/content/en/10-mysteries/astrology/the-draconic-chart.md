# The Draconic Chart#


A Draconic Natal Chart, from the lens of Yogācāra (a school of Buddhist philosophy focusing on consciousness and perception), becomes a map not of material or social karma (as in traditional tropical astrology), but of karmic vasanas—latent tendencies buried deep in the ālaya-vijñāna, the “storehouse consciousness.”



1. Draconic Chart as Storehouse Consciousness Map

In Yogācāra, your ordinary natal chart (tropical or sidereal) relates to the manovijñāna (ordinary cognitive awareness shaped by sensory and conceptual input). It’s the chart of the “person” you perceive yourself to be.



But the draconic chart—which is calculated by placing Ketu (South Node) at 0º Aries and recalibrating all other planetary positions—is a chart of substrate karmic patterns, prior to surface identity. It reflects the ālayavijñāna, the karmic seeds (bīja) before they sprout into lived experiences.



Draconic = “dragon” = Nāga = serpent = ancient subconscious memory = vasana blueprint.

Thus, the draconic chart is a Nāga-map of the self beneath the self.



2. Ketu at Aries: The Inceptive Flame

In this system, we are to set Ketu (South Node) at 0º Aries by default—this makes Aries the original spark of individuation in the karmic cycle. It’s the moment of ignition, a karmic fire:

	•	Aries is Mars-ruled, and in Yogācāra terms, Mars corresponds to tendencies toward action (karma formation) through volition (cetanā).

	•	Ketu is headless—blind instinct, root karma without thought.

	•	This represents the first movement of consciousness into samsaric reality, a flame arising from the ocean of potential.



In Campbell’s terms: the “call to adventure” before you know you’re on a journey.

Ketu here is the uroboric tail: unconscious karmic origin. You are not *born* at Ketu—you *emerge* from it.



3. Rahu as the Meeting with the Goddess

In the Yogācāra + Hero’s Journey synthesis:

	•	Rahu is desire, projection, illusion—but also encounter.

	•	In Campbell’s mythos, Rahu corresponds to the “Meeting with the Goddess” or sacred marriage—where the seeker’s ego is overwhelmed by a greater image, often feminine, often terrifying and beautiful.



Yogācāra interprets this moment as the confrontation with the aparāvidyā (lower, distorted perception) *becoming*parāvidyā (liberative wisdom)—if the mind is trained.



Rahu thus becomes not only delusion but the threshold guardian of transcendent gnosis.



Rahu is the Nāga head. He is what you see when the serpent turns toward you.

	•	His horseshoe shape, echoing the hood of the Indian King Cobra, is the double crescent of birth-death duality.

	•	On the back of the King Cobra’s head, this shape resembles eyes or a face—it’s a deceptive image meant to paralyze predators. So too, Rahu paralyzes the ego with his illusion… unless you can see through the image.



4. The Ouroboros and Return to Ketu

In this framing, the spiritual journey is circular:

	•	Begin at Ketu in Aries (primal fire of individuation),

	•	Traverse Rahu (the mirrored other, the goddess, the false self, the threshold),

	•	Return to Ketu, but now not as blind instinct—but as realization.



In Yogācāra terms, this is the transformation of the eight consciousnesses into the four wisdoms:

	•	The storehouse consciousness (ālaya-vijñāna) is purified into great mirror wisdom (mahādārśana-jñāna).

	•	The journey closes as Ketu becomes gnosis.



So Ketu returns—but now, no longer karmic inertia but the self-consuming flame of nirvāṇa.



5. Symbolism of the Cobra: Nāga and the Dual Nodes

The horseshoe symbol of Rahu/Ketu—a double crescent with open ends—derives from:

	•	The hood of the Indian King Cobra (Ophiophagus hannah), whose markings look like an eye or dual arches.

	•	The Nāga is both guardian of treasure and deceiver, just as Rahu is both disruptor and initiator.

	•	In esoteric Buddhism, Nāgas guard sacred texts and relics—mirroring how the nodes guard (and conceal) the wisdom in your karmic matrix.



By confronting Rahu, you decode the illusion. By returning to Ketu, you dissolve the illusion.
