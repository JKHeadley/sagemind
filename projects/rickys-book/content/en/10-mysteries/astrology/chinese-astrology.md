# Chinese Astrology



1. Core Structure: The Four Pillars of Destiny (BaZi 八字)

At its heart, Chinese astrology is calculated using a person’s birth year, month, day, and hour, producing four “pillars”:

	•	Year Pillar: Outer personality, ancestry, early childhood

	•	Month Pillar: Inner personality, family, early environment

	•	Day Pillar: Self and spouse (this pillar is considered the most personal)

	•	Hour Pillar: Children, dreams, legacy



Each pillar consists of:

	•	Heavenly Stem (10 total)

	•	Earthly Branch (12 total — corresponds to the zodiac animals)



2. The 12 Zodiac Animals (Earthly Branches 地支)

Each year in a 12-year cycle is ruled by one of these animals:

	1.	Rat (Zi) – clever, adaptable - 1984, 1996, 2008 etc.

	2.	Ox (Chou) – diligent, dependable - 1985, 1997, 2009 etc.

	3.	Tiger (Yin) – brave, confident - 1986, 1998, 2010 etc.

	4.	Rabbit (Mao) – kind, elegant - 1987, 1999, 2011 etc.

	5.	Dragon (Chen) – charismatic, powerful - 1988, 2000, 2012 etc.

	6.	Snake (Si) – wise, enigmatic - 1989, 2001, 2013 etc.

	7.	Horse (Wu) – energetic, independent - 1990, 2002, 2014 etc.

	8.	Goat (Wei) – gentle, creative - 1991, 2003, 2015 etc.

	9.	Monkey (Shen) – witty, curious - 1992, 2004, 2016 etc.

	10.	Rooster (You) – observant, hardworking - 1993, 2005, 2017 etc.

	11.	Dog (Xu) – loyal, just - 1994, 2006, 2018 etc.

	12.	Pig (Hai) – compassionate, generous - 1995, 2007, 2019 etc.



Each zodiac is associated with an element and Yin/Yang polarity.

4. Five Elements (Wu Xing 五行)

Each stem and branch corresponds to one of the Five Elements:

	•	Wood: growth, flexibility

	•	Fire: passion, transformation

	•	Earth: stability, nourishment

	•	Metal: structure, focus

	•	Water: wisdom, adaptability



These elements generate and control each other in cycles:

	•	Generating:

Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → (back to Wood)

	•	Controlling:

Wood → Earth → Water → Fire → Metal → (back to Wood)



5. Yin and Yang (阴阳)

Every stem and branch has a Yin or Yang aspect.

	•	Yang: active, outward, bright

	•	Yin: receptive, inward, dark



The dynamic interaction of Yin and Yang is fundamental in interpreting a chart.

6. Animal Combinations and Clashes

Zodiac animals interact with each other through:

	•	Trines (natural allies, forming harmonious triangles)

	•	Oppositions (clashes)

	•	Combinations (merge into another element)

	•	Punishments/Harms (disharmonies)



These influence personality, compatibility, and fate cycles.

7. Luck Pillars (Da Yun 大运)

In BaZi, 10-year luck cycles influence your fate. They start from a certain age depending on the chart and show which elements dominate during that period.



8. Additional Concepts

	•	Ten Gods (Shi Shen 十神): Derived from how other elements relate to the Day Master (self-element). They include:

	•	Direct Officer, Seven Killings – authority, discipline

	•	Wealth (Proper/Indirect) – resource acquisition

	•	Resource (Proper/Indirect) – learning, support

	•	Output (Eating God, Hurting Officer) – expression, creativity

	•	Companions (Friend, Rob Wealth) – competition, collaboration

	•	Zi Wei Dou Shu (紫微斗数): Another system akin to natal astrology, focused on star placements and palace divisions (more like Western astrology).

	•	Feng Shui Connection: Chinese astrology often intertwines with Feng Shui to assess and balance environmental energy flows.

	•	Taoist Cosmology & Ancestral Spirits: Some interpretations link BaZi to ancestral influence, spiritual destiny, and Taoist metaphysics.



9. Application Areas

	•	Personality Analysis: Understanding strengths, weaknesses, tendencies

	•	Compatibility: Between individuals (especially marriage, business)

	•	Career Guidance: Best elements and timing for success

	•	Timing of Events: Auspicious dates for action

	•	Fate Correction: Feng Shui, name changes, rituals to balance lacking elements
