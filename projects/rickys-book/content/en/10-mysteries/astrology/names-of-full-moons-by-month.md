# Names of Full Moons by Month



Here’s a full list of the traditional full moon names used throughout the year, primarily based on Native American, Colonial American, and European traditions (especially those of the Algonquin tribes). These names were often tied to seasonal changes, agricultural cycles, animal behavior, and climate.



1. January — Wolf Moon

	•	Meaning: Named for the howling of wolves, which was often heard during the deep snows of midwinter.

	•	Symbolism: Hunger, survival, introspection, primal instincts.



2. February — Snow Moon

	•	Meaning: The heaviest snowfalls typically occur in February.

	•	Symbolism: Stillness, endurance, purification, waiting for renewal.



3. March — Worm Moon

	•	Meaning: As the ground thaws, earthworms begin to appear, signaling spring’s arrival.

	•	Symbolism: Renewal, awakening, fertility.



*Alternate names: Crow Moon, Sap Moon (when maple sap begins to flow).*

4. April — Pink Moon

	•	Meaning: Named after the herb moss pink, or phlox, one of the earliest widespread spring flowers.

	•	Symbolism: Blossoming, youth, early growth.



*Alternate names: Sprouting Grass Moon, Egg Moon, Fish Moon (when fish swim upstream to spawn).*

5. May — Flower Moon

	•	Meaning: Flowers are abundant during May.

	•	Symbolism: Fertility, beauty, abundance, romance.



6. June — Strawberry Moon

	•	Meaning: This is when strawberries ripen and are traditionally harvested.

	•	Symbolism: Sweetness, fruition, relationships.



*Alternate names: Rose Moon, Honey Moon (also linked to weddings).*

7. July — Buck Moon

	•	Meaning: Buck deer begin to grow new antlers in July.

	•	Symbolism: Masculinity, strength, regeneration.



*Alternate names: Thunder Moon (for summer storms), Hay Moon.*

8. August — Sturgeon Moon

	•	Meaning: Sturgeon fish were most readily caught this time of year in the Great Lakes.

	•	Symbolism: Harvest, perseverance, maturity.



*Alternate names: Green Corn Moon, Grain Moon.*

9. September — Corn Moon or Harvest Moon

	•	Meaning: Coincides with the corn harvest; when the full moon is closest to the autumn equinox, it’s called the Harvest Moon.

	•	Symbolism: Gathering, completion, reward for labor.



10. October — Hunter’s Moon

	•	Meaning: After the harvest, it was time to hunt and store meat for winter.

	•	Symbolism: Preparation, sacrifice, foresight.



11. November — Beaver Moon

	•	Meaning: Time to set beaver traps before swamps froze, ensuring a supply of warm furs.

	•	Symbolism: Resourcefulness, hard work, survival.



*Alternate names: Frost Moon, Mourning Moon.*

12. December — Cold Moon

	•	Meaning: Marks the beginning of deep winter and long nights.

	•	Symbolism: Endings, stillness, reflection.



Special Moons:

Blue Moon

	•	Definition: The second full moon in a calendar month (rare).

	•	Symbolism: Rarity, magic, unpredictability.



Supermoon

	•	Definition: A full moon that appears larger because it’s at its closest point to Earth (perigee).

	•	Symbolism: Amplification of lunar energy, heightened emotion.



Blood Moon

	•	Definition: A total lunar eclipse, where the moon appears red.

	•	Symbolism: Omen, transformation, death/rebirth cycles.
