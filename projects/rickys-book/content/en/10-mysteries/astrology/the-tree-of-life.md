# The Tree of Life



In Kabbalah, the Tree of Life is a mystical diagram made up of spheres (called Sephirot) connected by paths. The amount of spheres and paths depends on the esoteric transmission one receives and tradition they subscribe to. Each sphere represents an attribute or emanation of God, from Keter (the crown, divine will) down to Malkuth (the kingdom, the physical world).

	•	It maps the path from the divine to the material world.

	•	Also used as a map of the soul, creation, and the process of enlightenment.

	•	Often overlaid with astrology, gematria, theophany, and alchemical symbolism.



In short it is a mnemonic device designed to help memorize several physical and metaphysical concepts all at once. It is geocentric meaning it must be looked at from earth’s perspective staring out at the sky with each sphere in order of distance from the earth. The horizontal bars within the tree are known as “Jacob’s Ladder.” Note that in some traditions like the Druze, the Archangel is the planet itself and they do not carry the western name. Also note that this tree is not to be confused with other cosmological trees such as the Norse Yggdrasil or Mayan Ceiba as these more accurately represent the celestial globe itself with the serpent at the root being the constellation Draco circling around the celestial pole and the top of the tree representing what the western world would consider the southern sky.
