# Mayan Astrology



I. Core Calendar Systems in Mayan Astrology

1. Tzolk’in Calendar (260 days)

	•	The most important calendar for Mayan astrology.

	•	Consists of 20 day signs (Nahuales) combined with 13 numbers (tones).

	•	Each day has a unique energy based on the combination of its day sign and tone (e.g., 7 Ajpu, 3 K’an).

	•	It’s a sacred cycle connected to spiritual energy, agricultural cycles, and human destiny.



2. Haab’ Calendar (365 days)

	•	Civil calendar composed of 18 months with 20 days each + a 5-day “nameless” period.

	•	Used for agricultural and ceremonial purposes.

	•	Sometimes used alongside the Tzolk’in for divinatory purposes.

3. Calendar Round

	•	A 52-year cycle that combines the Tzolk’in and Haab’.

	•	Each unique combination repeats every 52 years, considered a “century” in Mayan reckoning.

4. Long Count Calendar

	•	A linear count of days since a mythological creation date (August 11, 3114 BCE in the Gregorian calendar).

	•	Used to track large historical cycles, including the 13-Baktun cycle (notably ending in 2012).

IV. Personal Mayan Horoscope

A person’s Mayan chart includes:

	•	Day Sign (Nahual): Your primary archetype.

	•	Tone (Number): Your spiritual energy or path.

	•	Mayan Cross (Cruz Maya): A five-part chart based on neighboring day signs—comparable to a natal chart.

	•	Center: Your core self.

	•	Left: Past/ancestral support.

	•	Right: Future/guidance.

	•	Top: Destiny or higher self.

	•	Bottom: Grounding or Earth mission.



V. Planetary Deities and Venus Cycles

	•	The Maya closely tracked Venus—especially its morning star and evening star phases.

	•	Venus was tied to war, sacrifice, prophecy, and divine timing.

	•	Venus calendar cycles (584 days) were embedded in ritual warfare and elite power rituals.



VI. Spiritual Purpose of Mayan Astrology

Mayan astrology is not just predictive—it is initiatory. It’s used to:

	•	Understand your mission or destiny (k’ulbal).

	•	Harmonize your life with cosmic cycles.

	•	Heal ancestral karma and guide community through spiritual roles.

Mayan daykeepers (ajq’ij) use this knowledge to:

	•	Conduct ceremonies.

	•	Give initiations.

	•	Offer guidance during critical life stages.
