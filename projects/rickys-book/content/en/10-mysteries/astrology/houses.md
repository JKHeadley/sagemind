# Houses



1st House – House of Self

	•	Keywords: Identity, appearance, first impressions, how you approach life.

	•	Ruler: Mars

	•	Natural Sign: Aries

	•	Details: This house represents *you*—your personality, body, mannerisms, and how others perceive you upon meeting. It sets the tone for your entire birth chart (Ascendant or Rising Sign sits here).



2nd House – House of Possessions

	•	Keywords: Money, personal values, material assets, self-worth.

	•	Ruler: Venus

	•	Natural Sign: Taurus

	•	Details: Concerns your financial situation, what you value (physically and morally), and your ability to attract abundance. Also shows how you handle and relate to money.



3rd House – House of Communication

	•	Keywords: Communication, siblings, short travel, learning, thinking style.

	•	Ruler: Mercury

	•	Natural Sign: Gemini

	•	Details: Governs language, speech, writing, immediate surroundings, and relationships with siblings and neighbors. Also shows your mental faculties and learning preferences.



4th House – House of Home and Family

	•	Keywords: Roots, ancestry, home, emotional foundation, mother.

	•	Ruler: Moon

	•	Natural Sign: Cancer

	•	Details: Represents your private life, childhood, domestic situation, and psychological foundation. Often linked to the mother or nurturing figure. It’s also where you seek safety and emotional security.



5th House – House of Pleasure

	•	Keywords: Creativity, romance, children, fun, hobbies, self-expression.

	•	Ruler: Sun

	•	Natural Sign: Leo

	•	Details: This house rules joy, passion projects, dating, and artistic pursuits. It also represents risk-taking, including gambling and speculative ventures.



6th House – House of Health and Service

	•	Keywords: Work, daily routines, health, service, pets.

	•	Ruler: Mercury

	•	Natural Sign: Virgo

	•	Details: Concerned with your work life, discipline, and how you maintain your physical and mental health. It also shows your habits, coworkers, and how you handle responsibilities.



7th House – House of Partnerships

	•	Keywords: Marriage, partnerships, one-on-one relationships, contracts.

	•	Ruler: Venus

	•	Natural Sign: Libra

	•	Details: Opposite the 1st House, this house reflects your relationships with others. It’s about commitment, both in love and in business. Shows how you collaborate or mirror others.



8th House – House of Death and Transformation

	•	Keywords: Death, rebirth, shared resources, inheritance, sexuality, the occult.

	•	Ruler: Pluto (traditionally Mars)

	•	Natural Sign: Scorpio

	•	Details: Deals with intense, transformative experiences—birth, death, trauma, and regeneration. Also rules joint finances, debts, and hidden psychological forces.



9th House – House of Philosophy

	•	Keywords: Travel, higher education, philosophy, law, religion.

	•	Ruler: Jupiter

	•	Natural Sign: Sagittarius

	•	Details: Associated with long-distance travel (both physical and intellectual), spiritual beliefs, foreign cultures, and expansion of consciousness. It governs your worldview and moral compass.



10th House – House of Career and Public Image

	•	Keywords: Career, ambition, reputation, status, authority.

	•	Ruler: Saturn

	•	Natural Sign: Capricorn

	•	Details: Sits at the top of the chart (Midheaven/MC) and shows your highest aspirations, life goals, and how the world sees you professionally. Also linked to father or authority figures.



11th House – House of Community and Hopes

	•	Keywords: Friends, social groups, humanitarianism, dreams, technology.

	•	Ruler: Uranus (traditionally Saturn)

	•	Natural Sign: Aquarius

	•	Details: Represents your social circles, allies, and how you contribute to collective movements or causes. It’s also the house of long-term dreams and visions.



12th House – House of the Unconscious

	•	Keywords: Isolation, dreams, spirituality, secrets, hidden enemies.

	•	Ruler: Neptune (traditionally Jupiter)

	•	Natural Sign: Pisces

	•	Details: Associated with mysticism, the subconscious mind, karma, hidden suffering, institutions (like prisons or hospitals), and divine union. It can show hidden strengths and blind spots.
