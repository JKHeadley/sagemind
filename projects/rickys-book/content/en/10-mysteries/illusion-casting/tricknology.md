# Tricknology



Tricknology is a term that originated in Nation of Islam (NOI) teachings, particularly through the speeches of Elijah Muhammad and later popularized by figures like Malcolm X and groups such as Public Enemy in hip-hop culture. It’s a blend of “trick” and “technology” or “methodology,” and refers to the use of deceptive, manipulative, or cunning methods—especially by dominant systems or powers—to control, mislead, or exploit people, particularly People of Color and marginalized communities.



Core Meaning:

	•	Tricknology = deceptive systems of control + manipulation of truth

	•	It often refers to:

	•	Propaganda and misinformation

	•	Institutionalized racism masked as neutrality

	•	Cultural co-optation or distortion of history

	•	Psychological conditioning through media, education, or religion



Broader Cultural Use:

Outside of the NOI context, “tricknology” is sometimes used more generally to describe clever but morally questionable tactics, especially by governments, corporations, or elites.
