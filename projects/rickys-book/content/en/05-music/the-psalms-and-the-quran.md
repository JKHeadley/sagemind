# The Psalms and The Quran



To put it plainly: the Psalms and the Qur’an are not merely texts—they are encoded, living systems of sound, symbol, and structure, designed to transmit esoteric knowledge, channel spiritual power, and at times, mobilize political or even military action. They act beyond religious scripture as musical, esoteric and political technologies.



I. The Psalms (Tehillim): Songs of Power, Lament, and Strategy

The Psalms were never meant to be read silently. They were meant to be sung, chanted, or performed, often with lyres, harps, and percussion, and they exist within a ritualized musical matrix.



1. Musical Mnemonic and Devotional Use

	•	Each Psalm follows poetic structures with parallelism, repetition, and rhythm, making them easy to memorize and pass down orally.

	•	They encode theological, cosmological, and ethical principles using metaphor and poetic archetypes: shepherd and sheep, fortress and enemy, flood and salvation.

	•	Many Psalms end with “*Selah*,” which likely indicated a musical or meditative pause, or possibly a coded instruction for initiates.



2. Esoteric and Magical Use

	•	Kabbalistic traditions treat Psalms as a form of divine technology. Each verse is associated with specific angelic names, sefirot (emanations of God), or energies.

	•	Specific Psalms are used in amulets, exorcisms, healings, and initiatory rites, believed to hold intrinsic vibratory power.

	•	For example:

	•	Psalm 91 is known as the “exorcist’s psalm”.

	•	Psalm 119, an acrostic of the Hebrew alphabet, was used for meditative ascent and contemplation of divine law.



3. Political and War-Time Use

	•	Many Psalms were composed in contexts of war, exile, and rebellion, especially under Babylonian and later Roman rule.

	•	Psalm 2 and Psalm 144, for example, are calls to arms against kings and rulers who oppose the divine will.

	•	David, traditionally considered the author of many Psalms, was both a warrior and mystic-king, making the Psalms both military hymns and mystical utterances.



So, the Psalms are:

	•	Esoteric instructions, disguised as devotional song.

	•	Coded protest texts and royal declarations.

	•	Tools of initiation and power, operating on vibrational and symbolic levels.



II. The Qur’an: Sonic Revelation, Esoteric Geometry, and Political Revolution

1. Sonic Transmission and Sacred Sound

	•	The Qur’an was revealed orally, through sound. The Prophet Muhammad heard it, memorized it, and recited it—*Qur’an* literally means “Recitation.”

	•	Tajwīd, the science of Qur’anic recitation, is not just about pronunciation—it’s about intonation, tone, rhythm, and breath, creating a vibratory resonance that’s felt spiritually and physically.

	•	Recitation (tilāwah) is a form of ritualized performance, often inducing a meditative or ecstatic state (similar to Sufi sama).



2. Esoteric Layers and Symbolism (Bāṭin)

	•	Islamic traditions (especially Sufi, Ismaili, and Shia) distinguish between:

	•	Ẓāhir (outer meaning) and

	•	Bāṭin (inner, esoteric meaning).

	•	Verses are often symbolic: references to light, gardens, veils, water, scales, and stars are read as spiritual archetypes or metaphysical truths.

	•	The Qur’an’s numeric structure, letter frequencies, and geometric patterns have been studied for hidden codes, sacred geometry, and cosmological meaning (e.g., the 19-based system studied by Rashad Khalifa, or traditional abjad numerology).



3. Political Instrument and War-Time Text

	•	From the start, the Qur’an had a political function. It:

	•	Unifies tribes under a new ethical-spiritual system.

	•	Denounces corrupt rulers and oligarchs (e.g., Meccan elites).

	•	Justifies defensive and strategic warfare (with conditions for conduct).

	•	Surahs like:

	•	Surah Al-Anfal and Surah At-Tawbah directly address military strategy, morale, and divine legitimization of war.

	•	Surah Muhammad and Surah Al-Fath are politically coded texts that map visions of victory and prophecy.



Like the Psalms, the Qur’an:

	•	Was and is sung as part of sacred praxis.

	•	Contains multiple levels of meaning—outer law and inner gnosis.

	•	Served as a manual for community-building, and resistance to tyranny.
