# The Dharma Religion Underneath Abrahamic Religions



1. Dharma

*(Cosmic Law / Path / Truth)*

Yogācāra (Buddhism)

	•	Dharma = both “phenomena” (what appears in consciousness) and “path” (the practice that leads to awakening).

	•	It’s the moral and cognitive structure of reality, expressed through the Eightfold Path, leading to purification of the *ālaya-vijñāna* (storehouse consciousness).

	•	Real Dharma aligns with the mind’s transformation toward non-duality.



Christianity

	•	John 1:1 – “In the beginning was the Word [Logos]…”

	•	Logos = divine structure and reason—equivalent to Dharma as cosmic order.

	•	John 14:6 – “I am the Way, the Truth, and the Life.”

	•	Christ is the incarnate *Dharma*, the personification of the spiritual path.

	•	Romans 2:14–15 – The law is written on the heart, even without the Torah.



Islam

	•	*Dīn* = the eternal path of submission to Allah.

	•	*Sharī‘ah* (outer law), *ṭarīqah* (inner path), *ḥaqīqah* (mystical truth) reflect increasing degrees of Dharma.

	•	Qur’an 6:153 – “This is My path, perfectly straight.”



Judaism

	•	Torah = the divine teaching; law and narrative fused.

	•	Psalm 119:105 – “Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path.”

	•	Proverbs 3:6 – “In all your ways acknowledge Him, and He will make your paths straight.”

	•	*Halakha* (“the way”) is similar to Dharma as path-structure.



2. Karma

*(Moral Causality / Action and Consequence)*

Yogācāra

	•	Karma = mental and physical actions that plant seeds in the storehouse consciousness.

	•	These seeds ripen into future experiences and rebirths.

	•	Liberation is the cessation of karmic planting through insight and practice.

Christianity

	•	Galatians 6:7 – “Whatever a man sows, that shall he also reap.”

	•	Romans 2:6 – “God will repay each person according to what they have done.”

	•	Matthew 12:37 – “By your words you will be justified, and by your words you will be condemned.”

	•	Works and faith together shape one’s destiny (James 2:26).



Islam

	•	Qur’an 99:7–8 – “Whoever does an atom’s weight of good or evil shall see it.”

	•	Deeds are written by angels and revealed at judgment.

	•	*Niyyah* (intention) is crucial—similar to Yogācāra’s emphasis on mental volition.



Judaism

	•	Proverbs 11:18 – “The wicked earns deceptive wages, but the one who sows righteousness reaps a sure reward.”

	•	Deuteronomy 30:15 – “See, I have set before you today life and good, death and evil…”

	•	*Middah k’neged middah* – “measure for measure”—God repays actions in kind.

	•	Kabbalah: actions affect the spiritual worlds (*Olamot*) and the sefirotic balance.



3. Samsara

*(Cycle of Death and Rebirth / Existence in Delusion)*

Yogācāra

	•	Samsara arises from mistaken perception and karmic projection.

	•	Cycles continue because of ignorance embedded in the subconscious mind (*ālaya*).

	•	Liberation is purification of this storehouse.

Christianity

	•	Ecclesiastes 1:9 – “What has been will be again… there is nothing new under the sun.”

	•	Romans 8:20–22 – “Creation was subjected to futility… groans in travail until now.”

	•	Fallen time-bound existence mirrors samsara.

	•	Baptism and spiritual rebirth break the cycle.



Islam

	•	*Dunyā* is the temporal world of attachment and illusion.

	•	Qur’an 29:64 – “The life of this world is only amusement and diversion…”

	•	Sufis see *nafs* (lower soul) as trapping the self in cyclical delusion.



Judaism

	•	Life outside Eden is a realm of toil, pain, and moral struggle—resonant with samsara.

	•	Ecclesiastes 3:20 – “All go to the same place; all come from dust, and to dust all return.”

	•	*Gilgul ha-nefesh* (Kabbalistic reincarnation) is the soul’s cyclical journey to perfection.



4. Reincarnation / Soul’s Return

Yogācāra

	•	No permanent self transmigrates—only karmic continuity.

	•	The mind-stream continues until purification.

Christianity

	•	John 9:2 – “Who sinned, this man or his parents, that he was born blind?” implies pre-birth existence.

	•	Matthew 11:14 – John the Baptist is “Elijah who is to come.”

	•	Church Fathers like Origen taught reincarnation (*apokatastasis*).

	•	“Born again” (John 3:3) can be read as both spiritual and cyclical renewal.



Islam

	•	Orthodoxy denies reincarnation, but some Sufi traditions allow metaphorical or spiritual forms.

	•	Qur’an 2:28 – “You were dead, then He gave you life; then He will cause you to die, then bring you to life again…”

	•	Ibn ‘Arabi: the soul wears many forms (*mazāhir*).



Judaism

	•	*Gilgul* = soul transmigration taught in the Zohar and Lurianic Kabbalah.

	•	Each life refines aspects of the soul, repairing damage from prior lives (*tikkun ha-nefesh*).

	•	Job 33:29–30 – “God does all these things… to bring back his soul from the Pit…” (some interpret cyclically).

	•	Soul cycles until it fulfills all aspects of the Torah.



5. Eternal Life / Liberation / Union

Yogācāra

	•	Nirvana = non-dual awareness, cessation of karmic seeds.

	•	No rebirth, no self—only luminous clarity (*prabhāsvara* mind).

Christianity

	•	John 17:3 – “This is eternal life: to know You, the only true God.”

	•	Revelation 21:4 – “There shall be no more death or sorrow…”

	•	1 Corinthians 15:53 – Mortal puts on immortality.

	•	Eternal life = divine union, not just endless duration.



Islam

	•	Eternal life = *baqā’*, abiding in God after *fanā’* (ego dissolution).

	•	Qur’an 84:6 – “O man, you are laboring toward your Lord, and you will meet Him.”

	•	Paradise is both literal and mystical—a return to the Real.



Judaism

	•	*Olam ha-Ba* = the World to Come; either after death or after resurrection.

	•	Daniel 12:2 – “Many who sleep in the dust shall awake…”

	•	Kabbalah: eternal life = *devekut*, cleaving to the Infinite (*Ein Sof*).

	•	Psalm 16:11 – “In Your presence is fullness of joy; at Your right hand are pleasures forevermore.”
